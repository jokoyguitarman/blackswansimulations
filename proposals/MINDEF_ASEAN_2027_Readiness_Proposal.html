Executive Summary

In one page

MINDEF will host and secure the ASEAN 2027 Summit — a high-profile event presenting a concentrated set of security threats: armed attacks, threats to protected dignitaries, crowd emergencies, and coordinated assaults on venues.

Black Swan Simulations proposes to extend its already-validated simulation platform — the platform MINDEF reviewed and approved in our recent engagement — into a dedicated summit-readiness training environment. It allows MINDEF to rehearse multi-agency response to realistic, evolving crises in a safe, repeatable, and fully measurable setting, with detailed after-action analysis for every exercise.

We will deliver five new high-threat scenario capabilities built specifically around the summit threat picture, with the platform operational by the start of Q4 2026. This gives MINDEF a full live-use window before the summit, during which any issues encountered are captured and resolved by us under a committed support arrangement.

Context

The objective

Hosting an ASEAN Summit places MINDEF and its partner agencies under exceptional operational pressure. The threats are diverse and fast-moving — and the cost of an inadequate response is measured in lives and national reputation. Traditional preparation (tabletop exercises, full-scale field drills) is valuable but expensive, infrequent, hard to repeat, and difficult to measure objectively.

This programme gives MINDEF a repeatable, measurable, multi-agency rehearsal environment. Teams can run the same scenario many times, vary the conditions, practise coordination under realistic stress, and receive an objective after-action review every time — at a fraction of the cost and logistical burden of a live drill.

What We Will Build

New capabilities for summit readiness

The summit threat picture requires scenarios in which threats and crowds move and react in real time. We will first build a shared simulation engine, then deliver five scenarios on top of it.

The simulation engine core

A one-time foundational investment that powers all five scenarios — which is why the scenarios can be delivered efficiently rather than as five separate builds.

  • Autonomous moving threat actors — adversaries that move, navigate, seek targets and react to the response.
  • Continuous real-time simulation — the situation evolves moment to moment, not just at scripted points.
  • Crowd dynamics — realistic modelling of crowd density, panic spread and crush risk.

The five summit-threat scenarios

🎯

1 · Moving active shooter

A mobile armed threat moving through a venue, requiring coordinated containment and intervention by police and special forces while civilians are protected.

🏛️

2 · Coordinated attack on a session hall

A multi-attacker assault on an occupied venue, combining armed threat, hostage/barricade dynamics and evacuation under pressure.

🛡️

3 · Assassination attempt on a dignitary

A threat against a protected VIP, exercising close-protection response, threat interdiction and contingency movement.

🚗

4 · Diplomatic convoy blockade

A diplomatic convoy obstructed en route, requiring route management, crowd handling and safe extraction.

👥

5 · Event-ground stampede

A crowd-crush emergency on event grounds, exercising crowd management, flow control and mass-casualty response.

🧩

Tailored to the summit

Each scenario is authored around the actual summit venues, agencies and doctrines, in consultation with MINDEF.

How We Deliver

Delivery approach

We use an MVP-first approach: deliver a genuinely usable capability by the start of Q4 2026, then expand while MINDEF is already training on the platform. This maximises the live-use window before the summit and ensures issues are surfaced and fixed early.

Why this matters for MINDEF: rather than waiting for a single “big bang” delivery close to the summit, MINDEF gains hands-on use from Q4 2026. Every issue encountered in real exercises is recorded and resolved by us under the support arrangement — so the platform is battle-tested well before it is needed.

Commitment

Live support & bug resolution

From operational readiness (Q4 2026) through the summit, Black Swan provides a committed support service:

  • A single channel for MINDEF to report any issue encountered during exercises.
  • Logging, triage and prioritisation of every reported issue.
  • Rapid resolution of defects, with priority handling for anything affecting exercise delivery.
  • Regular status reporting so MINDEF always has visibility of platform health.

Specific response and resolution targets (service levels) will be agreed with MINDEF and confirmed in the contract.

Security & Deployment

Hosting, security & data residency

We recognise that a MINDEF capability carries strict requirements for security, personnel suitability and data residency. We will align fully with MINDEF’s requirements during M0, and propose the following principles:

  • Accredited environment. The platform is deployed into an environment that meets MINDEF’s accreditation and data-residency requirements (MINDEF-provided or jointly arranged).
  • Personnel suitability. Personnel handling MINDEF data and deployment meet MINDEF’s clearance and citizenship requirements.
  • Separation of concerns. General platform engineering is performed in a non-sensitive development environment; anything touching MINDEF data or the live environment is handled strictly within MINDEF’s approved boundary by suitable personnel.
Hosting and accreditation costs are not included in the programme price, as they depend on the environment MINDEF selects. Where MINDEF provides the environment, there is no such cost. Where Black Swan arranges it, it is billed at cost plus a modest handling charge, agreed in advance.
