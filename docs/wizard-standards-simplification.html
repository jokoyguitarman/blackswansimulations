The built-in standard

Your framework becomes the rubric

The internal doc's six attributes are encoded once into RESPONSE_STANDARDS (a ResearchGuidelines constant). Every social scenario now uses it automatically — no per-scenario research.

1 · Speed

Attribute 1 · Speed of Communication
  • Quick acknowledgement — beat the info vacuum
  • Accurate — verify before sharing
  • Consistent across touchpoints
freeze pre-scheduledfirst-strike

2 · Transparency

Attribute 2 · Rule of Three (Murphy 2015)
  • Tell the Truth
  • Tell it All
  • Tell it Fast
no canned messagesno plot holes

3 · Victim-Centered

Attribute 3 · Stakeholders > Shareholders
  • Victim cluster — low responsibility
  • Accidental — moderate
  • Preventable — high (apologize + compensate)
match empathy to cluster

4 · Ethical Posture

Attribute 4 · RDAP Scale
  • Reactive → Defensive
  • Accommodative → Proactive
  • Target: do more than required

Attribute 5 · Unified Voice

  • No message fragmentation

Deletions

What goes away — safely

Both removals were verified against the runtime. One is pure dead weight; the other is replaced, not lost.

deleted

Step 5 · Sentiment Research

Produced the sentiment_profile (analogous "similar incidents", reaction arc, demographics). The audits confirmed it's never read at runtime — only shown in the wizard. Deleting it has zero gameplay impact.

  • Removes /research-sentiment + researchPublicSentiment
  • Drops PublicSentimentProfile + the analogous-cases UI
  • One fewer AI round-trip
replaced

Step 6 · Best Practices research

research_guidelines is used at runtime (grader, SOP, cancel-gate), so it's replaced — sourced from your built-in standard instead of researched per scenario.

  • Removes /research-general + researchGeneralBestPractices
  • initial_state.research_guidelines = RESPONSE_STANDARDS
  • Faster, and grounded in your doctrine
