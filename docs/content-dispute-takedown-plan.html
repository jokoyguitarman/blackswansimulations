Implementation Plan / Preview

Fact-Based Takedown & Dispute System

Players can challenge a news article or social post they believe spreads misinformation by submitting counter-facts. An AI judge weighs their evidence against the scenario's ground-truth fact sheet and decides to retract, correct, or reject — with realistic delay, scoring, and notifications.

Adjudication: AI auto-judge Scope: Articles + Social Posts Ground truth: scenario.initial_state.fact_sheet New migration: 187 New service: contentDisputeService.ts
Design tie-in: Players are never shown the hidden is_factual / UNVERIFIED flag. The dispute system is exactly how they act on suspicion — they must read, evaluate, and commit. The AI verdict is the payoff that reveals whether they were right.

2

AI adjudication service

AI
New file
server/services/contentDisputeService.ts

The AI judge reads the pre-produced fact sheet (already saved by the warroom pipeline) and returns a structured verdict:

// Ground truth already stored at scenario.initial_state.fact_sheet
// { confirmed_facts: string[], unconfirmed_claims: [{claim, status, truth}] }
{
  "verdict": "uphold | correct | reject",
  "confidence": 0.0,
  "reason": "...",
  "correction_note": "..."
}

Judge rules: uphold only if content genuinely conflicts with confirmed facts AND the player's evidence is materially correct; correct if partially inaccurate; reject if the content was accurate or the player's "facts" are wrong.

Applies a realistic delay (60-180s), then updates content state, notifies the player, and scores the outcome — reusing the exact OpenAI + setTimeout pattern from npcEmailReplyService.ts.

Null fact-sheet fallback: Fact sheets live on the scenario, so sessions whose scenario wasn't generated by the social-crisis warroom may have none. In that case the judge leans toward "reject" / low confidence rather than inventing ground truth.

Scoring Logic
Player ActionIf content was actually falseIf content was actually accurate
Files a dispute with solid facts + Trust / fact-check credit (caught it) − Trust (frivolous, crying "fake news")
Files a dispute with weak/wrong facts Neutral / low impact − Trust (frivolous)
Does nothing (ignores misinformation) Missed opportunity (no fact-check credit) No effect

This mirrors real reputational risk: challenging accurate reporting wrongly damages your credibility, while catching genuine misinformation with evidence builds trust. The 60-180s delay and AI skepticism prevent instant, gamey takedowns.
