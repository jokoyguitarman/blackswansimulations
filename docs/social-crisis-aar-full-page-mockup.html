AI analysis · sentiment and turning points

The response did not recover because it posted more frequently; it recovered when the content became more specific. The inflection at T+35 followed the first post that named the affected batch range, gave a customer action, and committed to a next update. This aligns with the stored grade: accuracy 94, clarity 90 and persuasiveness 84. The earlier T+15 holding statement was appropriately cautious, but its failure to state the known geographic scope allowed the “all products” rumour to dominate for another 20 minutes.

AI analysis · crisis timeline

The session divides into three phases: awareness (T+0–15), information fragmentation (T+15–31), and coordinated recovery (T+31–60). The central training issue was not fact verification—the correct fact existed at T+8—but transmission. The public penalty at T+25 therefore reflects an internal coordination failure rather than a messaging failure alone.

The team should establish a rapid review channel that preserves Communications’ ownership of public language while surfacing risk before release.

AI analysis · information sharing

The exercise’s most valuable coordination lesson is that possession of a verified fact did not translate into organisational knowledge. Procurement’s verification was timely, but the cross-team handoff was not. Legal demonstrated the stronger pattern: it shared the regulator constraint at T+18, before Communications made a remedy claim, preventing a second negative consequence. Future runs should treat verified facts as handoff tasks, not merely inbox tasks.

07 · Takeaways

Prioritised recommendations

The final synthesis call reads all preceding section analyses and converts them into concrete behaviours for the next exercise.

5 actions
10
Turn verified facts into explicit cross-team handoff tasks.

Every team that verifies a fact should identify who else needs it and send it immediately. See Information flow.

Use a minimum viable holding-statement checklist.

Include confirmed scope, immediate public action, known unknowns and the time of the next update. See Crisis timeline.

Prioritise misinformation by reach and decision harm.

Directly answer the highest-reach harmful claim before lower-impact comment threads. See Misinformation.

Establish a rapid Legal–Communications review channel.

Legal should surface risk before publication without taking ownership of public language. See Team deep-dives.

Track recovery by specificity, not posting volume.

The decisive recovery followed the most specific statement, not the busiest period of posting. See Sentiment journey.
