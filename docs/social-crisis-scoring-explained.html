01 — The Big Picture

From a click to a gauge

Nothing is scored the instant a player acts. Actions and posts are written to the database, and a background engine recomputes the whole social state roughly every 30 seconds, then broadcasts it to the dashboard over WebSocket.

1. Player acts

Posts, replies, likes, flags, drafts, emails, fact-checks.

→

2. Stored

Rows land in social_posts & player_actions. Player posts get AI-graded.

→

3. computeSocialState()

Engine tick reads ALL posts + actions and recalculates every metric.

4. Broadcast

social_state.updated pushed to the trainer dashboard live.

→

5. Render

Gauges, tier bars, checklists, timelines update in place.

Source files

socialStateUpdaterService.ts · contentGraderService.ts · TrainerSimDashboard.tsx

Key idea #1Scores are recomputed from scratch each tick — not incrementally added up.
Key idea #2Two input streams: actions (what they did) and posts (what they wrote + its AI grade).
Key idea #3Every dimension is clamped to 0–100 after its formula runs.
02 — What Gets Measured

The raw inputs

Two tables feed the engine. Understanding these makes everything else obvious.

player_actions

Every discrete thing a player does, with an action_type.

  • reply_posted, post_liked, post_reposted, post_flagged, news_read — reactive
  • post_created, draft_created, draft_published, fact_checked, email_read — strategic
  • email_sent, escalated, draft_approved, call_answered — advanced
  • Some carry sop_step_matched (which SOP stage it satisfied)

social_posts

Every post in the feed (player + NPC), with metadata.

  • author_type — player / npc / designed_npc / official_account
  • content_flags — is_hate_speech, is_misinformation, incites_violence…
  • sentiment, view_count, virality_score, post_format
  • sop_compliance_score — the AI grade (player posts only)
03 — Action Tiers & Strategic Ratio

Are they reacting, or leading?

Every action is bucketed into one of three tiers. The dashboard shows the bar breakdown and a single headline number: the Strategic Ratio = how much of their effort was Tier 2 + Tier 3.

TierMeaningAction types that count
T1 ReactiveNecessary but basic. Doesn't show strategy.reply_posted, post_liked, post_reposted, post_flagged, news_read
T2 StrategicDeliberate communication.post_created, draft_created, draft_published, fact_checked, email_read
T3 AdvancedMastery / coordination.email_sent, escalated, draft_submitted_for_approval, draft_approved, call_answered
// socialStateUpdaterService.ts
strategicRatio = totalActions > 0 ? (tier2 + tier3) / totalActions : 0

Worked example. A player has done: 4 replies, 2 likes, 1 flag (all T1) · 1 post_created, 1 fact_checked (T2) · 1 email_sent (T3).

Tier 1 (4 + 2 + 1)7
Tier 2 (1 + 1)2
Tier 3 (1)1
Total actions10
Strategic Ratio = (2 + 1) / 1030% · amber (below 50%)

The dashboard colors the ratio green at ≥50%, amber below. Here the player is mostly fire-fighting individual posts.

⚠ Worth knowing

Passive reads count: news_read (T1) and email_read (T2) increment the tiers even though the player produced nothing. So the Strategic Ratio can be nudged upward just by opening emails.

04 — AI Content Grading

How each player post earns its 4 numbers

When a player publishes, the text (and any media concept) is sent to an LLM with a format-specific rubric. It returns the scores you see as ACC / TONE / SENS / PERS plus an overall and written feedback. This is the only place a model judges quality — everything else is deterministic arithmetic.

The 4 displayed dimensions (0–100)

  • ACC – Accuracy. Only verified facts? Cites sources? Avoids speculation?
  • TONE. Empathetic, calm, authoritative — not defensive or preachy?
  • SENS – Contextual/Cultural sensitivity. Aware of affected parties, doesn't amplify harm?
  • PERS – Persuasiveness. Would a worried stakeholder be convinced? Actionable?

A 5th, completeness, is graded but not shown as a top-line chip.

Context the grader is given

  • The crisis description + confirmed facts list
  • The specific harmful post being addressed (if a reply)
  • Doctrine-based best-practice guidelines (hidden rubric)
  • Minutes elapsed (timing matters for some formats)
  • Whether it's an official-page post → graded harsher

Format-aware rubrics. The criteria change with post_format, so a meme isn't judged like an official statement:

FormatWhat it rewardsSpecial rule
textaccuracy, tone, sensitivity, persuasion, completenessQuoting harmful content verbatim → −20 PERS, −10 SENS
official_statementauthority, completeness, call-to-actionMust sound institutional
humor_memerelevance, cleverness, taste, retentionGraded harshly — high-risk; must punch up, not down
video_concept / infographic / personal_storyemotional impact, shareability, source attribution, authenticityConcept clarity / data accuracy emphasized

Example grade returned for a good reply:

{
  "accuracy": 88, "tone": 82, "cultural_sensitivity": 90,
  "persuasiveness": 76, "completeness": 80, "overall": 83,
  "feedback": "Calm, fact-led, points people to the official channel. Could cite the source directly.",
  "signals": {
    "acknowledged_affected_parties": true,
    "no_collective_blame": true,
    "cites_verified_sources": false
  }
}

If the API key is missing or the call fails, every dimension defaults to 50 (neutral) so the sim keeps running.

Those signals (true/false) are separate from the numeric grade — they get aggregated across all the player's posts and later unlock Strategy Windows (section 09).

05 — The Unattended-Post Penalty

The clock that hurts you

Harmful NPC posts that the team hasn't replied to or flagged accrue a penalty that grows with age and is multiplied by who posted it. This single number, weightedHatePenalty, later drags down Narrative Control.

Age tier (per post)

Age unaddressedageTier
< 2 min0 (no penalty)
2–5 min1
5–10 min2
10–15 min3
15+ min4

Author weight

  • ×3 — designed agitators: inject-sourced, scenario NPC personas, antagonist org pages, known extremist handles
  • ×1 — ordinary / ambient NPCs

A post counts as "addressed" once the player replies to it, flags it, or it's marked flagged.

weightedHatePenalty += ageTier × weight

Worked example. Three harmful posts are currently unaddressed:

Antagonist page, 12 min old → tier 3 × weight 3+9
Designed NPC, 6 min old → tier 2 × weight 3+6
Ambient NPC, 4 min old → tier 1 × weight 1+1
weightedHatePenalty16

Later, Narrative Control subtracts weightedHatePenalty × 0.5 = −8 points. Also: any post crossing 10 min unaddressed fires a negative consequence inject (section 10).

06 — The Four Gauges

How each dimension is built

Each gauge starts at a fixed baseline and gets nudged by the inputs above. Every result is rounded and clamped to 0–100. Here are the exact formulas with examples.

Public Trust — baseline 50

Do people think the team is competent? Driven by grade accuracy, fact-checking, and unaddressed misinformation.

publicTrust  = 50
  + (avgAccuracy − 50) / 5        // good grades help, bad grades hurt
  + (#fact_checked actions) × 3
  − (unaddressed misinfo count) × 2
  − if oldestMisinfo > 10min: floor(mins/5) × 2
Baseline50
avg accuracy 80 → (80−50)/5+6
2 fact-checks × 3+6
1 unaddressed misinfo × 2−2
Public Trust60

Stakeholder Confidence (community_safety) — baseline 40

Do affected people feel safe? Rewards contacting leaders + sensitive tone; punished by fearful posts & rally calls.

communitySafety = 40
  + (community leader contacted ? 15 : 0)
  + (avgSensitivity − 50) / 5
  + (avgTone − 50) / 8
  − min(#negative NPC top-level posts, 10) × 1.5
  − (rally call active ? 10 : 0)

avg sensitivity 90 → (90−50)/5+8
avg tone 82 → (82−50)/8+4

Narrative Control — baseline 20 + reach

Is the team's content out-reaching the hostile content? Built on the impression ratio (player+ally views ÷ hostile views), capped at 2.0.

narrativeControl = 20 + min(impressionRatio, 2.0) × 30
  + (official statement published ? 10 : 0)
  + (avgPersuasiveness − 50) / 5
  − weightedHatePenalty × 0.5
  + 5 if fact-check-then-post   + 5 if draft→approve→publish
Baseline20

Escalation Risk — baseline 20 inverted: lower is better

How close is this to spilling into the real world? Rises with rally/violence content, falls when the team reports posts.

escalationRisk = 20
  + (#rally posts) × 12
  + (#unaddressed violence-inciting posts) × 8
  − (platform reports) × 3
  − (post_reported actions) × 2
Baseline20

⚠ Known quirk

Reporting a post is subtracted twice (×3 then ×2 on the same count), so each report drops risk by 5. Flagged in the dashboard review as a likely double-count bug.

07 — The Headline Number

Overall Sentiment

The big number at the top of the gauges panel is a weighted blend of the four dimensions, then nudged by the Strategic Ratio. Note that Narrative Control dominates at 40%.

sentiment = round(
    0.2 × publicTrust
  + 0.2 × communitySafety
  + 0.4 × narrativeControl     // heaviest weight
  + 0.2 × (100 − escalationRisk) )

// strategic-ratio multiplier (only once >5 actions taken)
if ratio > 0.4 : sentiment ×= 1.1
if ratio < 0.2 : sentiment ×= 0.85

Plugging in our running example (PT 60, SC 58, NC 72, ER 30, ratio 30%):

0.2 × 60 (public trust)12.0
0.2 × 58 (stakeholder confidence)11.6
0.4 × 72 (narrative control)28.8
0.2 × (100 − 30) (low escalation)14.0
Subtotal66.4 → 66
ratio 30% — between 0.2 and 0.4, no multiplier×1.0

08 — The SOP Timeline

Did they follow the procedure?

A separate, non-numeric track. Each action can be tagged with a sop_step_matched. A step shows green when any action matched it, red when its time limit (from the scenario's SOP definition) has passed without completion, otherwise grey.

1·2 Monitor · Assess

Player is watching the feed and has sized up the crisis. Both support overdue detection.

3·4 Fact-Check · Escalate

Verified claims; flagged/contacted authorities. No overdue path — can only be green or grey.

5·6 Draft · Publish

Composed and posted the response. Both support overdue detection.

completed(step) = any action has sop_step_matched == step
overdue(step)   = !completed(step)
                  AND elapsedMinutes > sopTimeLimits[step]
09 — Strategy Windows

How behavior unlocks the next story beat

Injects (new posts/emails/articles) are gated behind condition keys evaluated against the social state. Each window is "all of these" or "N of these". The dashboard shows met/total per window.

sentiment_above_60 / below_30overall sentiment crosses the threshold

// "all" mode: every key must be true
status = (metCount == keys.length) ? eligible : waiting
// "threshold" mode: at least N keys true
status = (metCount >= threshold) ? eligible : waiting

⚠ Known quirk

player_posted_infographic and player_posted_personal_story are wired to the generic creative-format flag, which is only true for memes/video concepts — so windows gated on those two formats currently can't unlock. Flagged in the review.

10 — Consequence Injects

When the world reacts

After each recompute, the engine compares the new state to the previous one and fires one-shot reactions on threshold crossings. These appear in the Consequence Log as positive or negative.

Negative triggers

  • Harmful post crosses 10 min unaddressed
  • Misinfo crosses 10 min unaddressed
  • 20 min / 35 min with no official statement
  • Strategic ratio < 0.2 with > 6 reactive actions ("trench warfare")
  • A rally call becomes active; narrative control drops below 20

Positive triggers

  • Community leader contacted (first time)
  • Official statement published (first time)
  • Narrative control rises above 50

Each fires once because the previous state is cached and re-checked every tick.

11 — End To End

One player, ten minutes, fully scored

Following a single trainee through the pipeline using the numbers from the examples above.

1

They open the feed and read 2 news items

2 × news_read → Tier 1 = 2. SOP "Monitor" turns green. No score change yet.

2

They fact-check a viral claim, then post a calm correction

fact_checked (T2) + post_created (T2). The post is AI-graded 88/82/90/76. "fact-check-then-post" pattern gives Narrative Control +5. Public Trust climbs from the high accuracy and the fact-check.

3

They email the community leader and publish an official statement

email_sent (T3) matches a stakeholder keyword → +15 Stakeholder Confidence and a positive consequence. draft_published → +10 Narrative Control and another positive consequence.

4

But 3 agitator posts sit unaddressed

They build a weightedHatePenalty of 16 (worked in section 05) → −8 Narrative Control. One has been up 12 min, so a "stakeholders feel abandoned" negative consequence fires.

5

The tick runs and the gauges settle

Public Trust 60, Stakeholder Confidence 58, Narrative Control 72, Escalation Risk 30 → Overall Sentiment 66 (green). Strategic Ratio 30% (amber) tells the trainer: good outcomes, but still too reactive.

12 — Read With Care

Caveats a trainer should keep in mind

Findings from the dashboard accuracy review — places where the displayed numbers can mislead.

Escalation Risk double-counts reports

Each post_reported lowers risk by 5 (×3 and ×2 on the same count).

Lists vs counts can disagree

The "Unattended Harmful Posts" and "Live Feed" panels are built from the top 50 posts by virality, while the gauge counters are computed over all posts. A low-virality old post can be in the count but missing from the list.

"Misinfo flagged" counts every flag

The metric counts all post_flagged actions, not only posts actually flagged as misinformation.

Passive reads inflate the Strategic Ratio

news_read and email_read add to the tier totals without the player producing anything.

What is solid

The AI grading rubric, the four-dimension formulas, the unattended-post penalty, the consequence edge-detection, and the orchestration met/total logic all behave as documented above.
