A Background & Objective

Context clauses — retain as written; they frame the engagement correctly.

KeepSOW § Background

"BSS requires a geospatial mapping capability that can convert external map and building data into a usable two-dimensional exercise environment." — engaging an external Consultant for selected geospatial and infrastructure components.

✅
Accurate framing. The platform already converts external map data to a 2-D environment; the Consultant supplements specific components rather than building the capability wholesale.
ReframeSOW § Objective

Enable BSS to: identify and convert OpenStreetMap building data into usable building outlines; integrate building blueprints, floor plans or site plans with the map environment.

✏️
Objective 1 (OSM→outlines) is already ~75% delivered in-house — reframe as "standardise & package existing extraction." Objective 2 (blueprint integration) is the genuine target — keep it front and centre.

1 Workstream 1 — OpenStreetMap to Building Outline

Engine exists and is battle-tested (osmVicinityService.ts, msBuildingFootprints.ts, geocodingService.ts, geoUtils.ts). Cut the rebuild; reframe to packaging.

CutWS1 § Scope / Required Activities

"Query and extract relevant OpenStreetMap building and road data within that radius" — including the core querying, failover and footprint logic.

🔁
Already built. Overpass extraction for buildings and roads with 5-endpoint failover, retry/backoff, multipolygon handling and a Microsoft Building Footprints fallback all exist. Hand over the code; do not commission a rebuild.
CutWS1 § Required Activities — location input

"Accept an identified location as an input" (coordinates or location reference).

🔁
Already built. Functions accept lat/lng directly, and geocodingService.ts already resolves place names → coordinates via Nominatim. Only a thin "location reference" wrapper would be net-new.
ReframeWS1 § Required Activities — radius

"Define a five-kilometre extraction radius around the input location."

✏️
Radius is fully parameterised today (300 m–10 km) but never fixed at 5 km, and the per-building path is hard-capped at 1 km. Reframe as: "standardise default to 5 km and lift the 1 km building cap."
ReframeWS1 § Outputs — reusable script & format

"A reusable extraction script or automated workflow… export the resulting building and road layers in JSON or other agreed formats." Deliver as standalone CLI + standard GeoJSON FeatureCollection (EPSG:4326).

✏️
Logic is currently embedded in the Express app + DB pipeline and outputs bespoke [lat,lng] JSON. Reframe to: "wrap existing functions as a standalone runner and emit RFC-7946 GeoJSON with separate building/road layers." This is cheap glue, not engineering.
KeepWS1 § Outputs — metadata, config & quality note

"Metadata files recording the input location, extraction radius, data source, extraction date, coordinate reference system, output format and any relevant OpenStreetMap attribution"; a "configuration template"; and a "brief data-quality note."

✅
Genuinely missing — keep. No CRS declaration, extraction date, or OSM/ODbL attribution is captured today, and there's no config template or compiled data-quality note (only transient fetch logs). This is the legitimate remainder of WS1.

2 Workstream 2 — Blueprint Integration with Maps

The core of the engagement. ~70% greenfield — mostly Keep. Display/schema scaffolding exists (scenario_floor_plans, FloorSelector, FloorPlanOverlay, feature taxonomy), so a few sub-clauses reframe.

KeepWS2 § Required Activities — ingestion

"Ingest blueprint, floor-plan or site-plan files provided by BSS in agreed formats, which may include PDF, image files, CAD-derived exports or other compatible formats."

✅
Entirely missing — keep. There is no upload/ingestion endpoint for plans at all. plan_image_url exists in the schema but nothing populates it. This is the heart of the engagement.
KeepWS2 § Required Activities — georeferencing

"Align each floor or level to the relevant building outline or site map using georeferencing, scaling, rotation and positioning methods."

✅
Missing — keep (highest value). Today there is only a crude bounding-box stretch — no rotation, affine/Helmert transform, control points or scale solver. Rigorous georeferencing is the single most valuable deliverable.
KeepWS2 § Required Activities — discrepancy flagging

"Flag discrepancies between the building outline derived from map data and the blueprint-derived building layout, including differences in scale, orientation, footprint, floor arrangement or missing information."

✅
Missing entirely — keep. Nothing compares a blueprint footprint to the OSM/map outline. This validation step is net-new and genuinely useful.
KeepWS2 § Required Activities — vertical connectivity

"Link vertical movement points, including staircases, lifts, escalators or other connectors, across relevant floors or levels."

✅
Mostly missing — keep. A Stairwell.connectsFloors field exists in the RTS types, but the multi-floor overlay has no enforced cross-floor linkage graph. Keep the connectivity-graph work.
ReframeWS2 § Required Activities — feature digitisation

"Digitise or extract agreed internal features from each floor: walls, rooms, corridors, entrances, exits, emergency exits, staircases, lifts, access routes, restricted areas."

✏️
The feature taxonomy & icons already exist (floorPlanSvgService.ts: emergency_exit, exit, escalator, elevator, stairs, corridor, room…) and a hand-draw editor exists in SceneEditor.tsx. Reframe to: "extract features into the existing taxonomy/schema" — don't redefine the model, populate it.
ReframeWS2 § Required Activities — floor hierarchy & toggling

"Maintain a clear floor-level hierarchy… produce editable geospatial layers that allow BSS to view, toggle, review and update building layouts by floor or level."

✏️
Multi-floor display + floor switcher already exist (FloorSelector.tsx understands B-levels/ground/upper floors; per-floor filtering in MapView). Reframe to "output into the existing floor-hierarchy model"; the multi-floor overlay is currently read-only, so per-floor editing UI is BSS-owned (see §C).
ReframeWS2 § Required Activities — export & metadata

"Export the processed multi-level blueprint layers in agreed formats… editable output files in JSON… metadata files recording source blueprint, ingestion date, floor/level id, alignment assumptions, CRS, processing notes."

✏️
Reframe export to the standard GeoJSON FeatureCollection contract (see §D) with properties.floor_level. Metadata/provenance is missing today — keep the metadata sub-requirement; reframe only the format wording.
BSS-ownsWS2 — gotchas to disclose, not bill

Resolving the two un-unified interior models (scenario_floor_plans.features vs rts_scene_configs interior arrays) and the dormant/hardcoded-off AI floor-plan generator.

🏛️
Tell the Consultant which model to target (rts_scene_configs is the more "live" path) up front. Choosing/normalising the canonical interior model is an architectural decision BSS should own, not delegate.

3 Technical Requirements

Mostly keep as guardrails; one critical insertion about the delivery format and one BSS-owned item.

BSS-ownsTech Req — platform compatibility

"Are compatible with the BSS simulation platform or agreed development environment." Deliver one GeoJSON FeatureCollection in EPSG:4326 (standard [lng,lat]) with a properties.layer discriminator.

🏛️
There is no GeoJSON/KML/shapefile importer in the product today — the import shim is BSS-owned, net-new work. Compatibility is achieved by mandating the standard format below, not by asking the Consultant to write into our JSONB tables (whose coordinate ordering is internally inconsistent).
KeepTech Req — editability, separation, attribution

Outputs must be editable/versionable; keep clear separation of raw vs processed vs simulation-ready data; preserve source attribution & licence metadata; be suitable for downstream evacuation/traffic/hazard/responder modules.

✅
Keep as standard quality guardrails. Note: the downstream evacuation, hazard and responder modules already exist — "suitable for integration with" is correct, but do not let it expand into building those modules.
KeepTech Req — no sensitive data

"Do not contain classified, restricted or sensitive operational information unless expressly approved by BSS in writing."

✅
Keep verbatim — important compliance guardrail given the bombing/evacuation subject matter.

5 Acceptance Criteria

Keep most; tighten two so acceptance is tested against our actual import path and tolerances.

KeepAcceptance — import & alignment

"Building outlines… can be imported into the BSS simulation environment"; "blueprint overlays are aligned to the relevant building outlines within an agreed tolerance."

✅
Keep — but define the tolerance numerically (e.g. ≤2 m positional, ≤3° rotational) and test against the GeoJSON contract + BSS import shim, not a hand-wave.
ReframeAcceptance — threat objects

"Threat, hazard and scenario objects are populated and can be displayed, edited and referenced within the agreed map environment."

✏️
Display/edit/reference of threat objects already works. Reframe acceptance to: "delivered threat zones import via the GeoJSON contract and render on the existing layers" — don't re-test capability we already own.

6 Responsibilities, Exclusions & Commercial Clauses

Mostly standard boilerplate — keep. A few items need a BSS action or a tightening.

BSS-owns§ BSS Responsibilities

BSS to provide site locations, available blueprints/floor plans, "required data schemas, platform interface requirements or sample data structures," feedback, and dev/test access.

🏛️
Action for BSS: hand over (a) the GeoJSON FeatureCollection contract, (b) the existing schema docs for scenario_floor_plans / rts_scene_configs, and (c) the existing OSM extraction code as the WS1 baseline. Doing this well is what prevents the Consultant rebuilding what we have.
Keep§ Consultant Responsibilities

Reasonable skill & care; qualified geospatial personnel; version control; protect confidential data; flag data gaps/risks; avoid restrictive third-party tools; deliver in editable formats.

✅
Keep verbatim. The "avoid restrictive third-party data/tools" clause matters — ensures deliverables stay reusable in our OSM/MS-footprint pipeline.
Keep§ Exclusions

Excludes engineering-certified blast/fire/structural modelling, certified safety assessment, classified data, live-system integration, real-time sensor integration, unlimited revisions/sites, paid datasets, and "development of the full BSS platform beyond the geospatial workstreams."

✅
Keep — and lean on it. The last exclusion is the firewall that stops scope creep into our existing evacuation/bombing/hazard engines. Pair it with the cut deliverables so "populate threat layers" can't balloon into "build the sim."
Keep§ IP & Usage Rights / Confidentiality

BSS retains pre-existing IP & platform architecture; Consultant assigns/licenses work products to BSS; identifies open-source/third-party components; treats site plans & security-relevant data with heightened care.

✅
Keep verbatim. The "BSS retains pre-existing platform architecture" wording explicitly protects the extraction engine, schema and sim code the Consultant is told to reuse.
Keep§ Project Management / Change Control

Periodic progress updates (completed / in-progress / risks / data-quality / decisions needed); interim demos; changes to sites, complexity, formats or deliverables subject to mutual agreement with cost/timeline impact.

✅
Keep. Change-control is the lever that lets BSS formally remove the cut items (threat schema, sim engines) from the agreed deliverables without dispute.

Σ Bottom line

How the redline changes the engagement

The SOW as written would pay for a substantial rebuild of capability we already own (OSM extraction, threat/hazard schema, evacuation & bombing sim). Redlined, the Consultant's hours concentrate on Workstream 2 — real blueprint ingestion and rigorous georeferencing — with Workstream 1 reduced to packaging and Workstream 3's overlap cut entirely. BSS retains the import shim, the unified layer UI, and the canonical interior-model decision as internal work.
