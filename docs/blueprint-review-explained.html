Alignment (open vocabulary)

4. What [NEXT] does — the generation step

Pressing [NEXT] moves you to Building, which runs a chain of generation calls, each receiving the blueprint, then Compile, which saves the finished scenario. Here is the exact sequence:

flowchart TD next["[NEXT] from Blueprint Review"] --> npc["1. NPCs + Fact Sheet + Communities (honors your 13 factions)"] npc --> story["2. Storyline injects (bucketed into your 10 stages)"] story --> conv["3. Convergence: narrative, objectives, dimension labels, gates"] conv --> org["4. Org page identity"] org --> compile["5. Compile + persist (blueprint saved to the scenario)"] compile --> ready["Scenario ready to run"]
  1. Characters & fact sheet. Because your factions coverage is high, the generator runs its "honor" path: it creates personas per faction, tagging each with its faction_id and alignment, distributed across your 13 groups — plus a large background cast so the feed feels real. It also builds the fact sheet (confirmed facts + false claims) and the affected communities.
  2. Storyline. It generates the timed injects (posts, emails, calls) that make up the crisis arc, ordered along your 10-stage timeline and seeded with your narrative mutations as misinformation.
  3. Convergence. It writes the scenario narrative/briefing, measurable objectives, the four scoring-dimension labels tailored to this crisis, and the condition-based "convergence gates" that fire when players do (or don't) act.
  4. Org page. It builds the organization's social identity/history for the simulated platform.
  5. Compile & persist. Everything is assembled and saved as a scenario — and the blueprint is stored on the scenario itself so the live engines can read it later.

6. What happens in the live session

Once trainees run the scenario, the blueprint keeps working — this is the Phase 5 runtime layer:

  • Scenario Director. On a bounded cadence it reads the blueprint + the live situation and makes one of your agitator factions act in-character for the current stage — escalating when things are tense, going quiet when players respond well. For this scenario that means Jihadist, Far-Right, Marxist, Islamophobic-resident, political, and disinformation beats appear over time, paced to the 10-stage arc.
  • Extremist hive. Adopts your lead hostile faction's narratives as its "wedge," so its divisive posts reflect your document's grievance rather than a generic one.
  • Safety, always. Every agitator post stays recognizable-but-not-operational: no real orgs/ideologies/slogans, no calls to violence — the 4chan link and literal slogans from your doc never surface as content.
Reward loop: the Director reads the same scoring signals players move. Clear, empathetic, timely official communication raises trust and narrative control, which makes the agitator factions act less — so good crisis-comms by the trainees visibly calms the simulated firestorm.
