Prison Siege to Caliphate Declaration
Strategic Frame
This exercise examines how localized tactical success, when combined with population coercion, ideological mobilisation, regional synchronisation, and foreign fighter inflows, can generate a rapid morale cascade among fragmented jihadist groups, producing consolidation effects that overwhelm conventional counterterrorism timelines.
The AI engine controls insurgent decision-making, morale shifts, recruitment dynamics, and propaganda exploitation, while retaining freedom to deviate tactically so long as it pursues the strategic trajectory from prison siege → consolidation → transnationalisation → urban siege.

Exercise Opening State
A coordinated prison assault occurs at a neighbourhood detention facility.Attackers:
Kill police officers on duty.
Breach cell blocks.
Free inmates, selectively recruiting ideologically primed detainees.
Seize automatic rifles, machine guns, and ammunition.
Withdraw into surrounding communities.
The state response is delayed and fragmented, while the local population initially displays overwhelming passive and active support.
Participants are informed that:
Some villages shelter militants for ideological-religious reasons.
Others comply due to fear, coercion, or economic dependency.
Cash, food, fuel, and intelligence are voluntarily or involuntarily provided.
Protests emerge alleging discrimination, abuse, and collective punishment.
The AI tracks population sentiment as a dynamic variable, not a binary condition.
Phase 1: Prison Siege
Disseminates propaganda framing the siege as divine vindication.
Portrays freed prisoners as “returned mujahidin.”
Exploits funerals of slain police to inflame grievances.
Tests state red lines through small armed probes.
Phase 2: Insurgent Coordination
Fighters migrate internally within Mindanao toward the initial siege node.
AI Options (Non-Linear):
Immediate second prison siege.
Ambush of responding security forces to seize weapons.
Targeted assassinations to paralyse local governance.
Regardless of path, the AI is incentivised to achieve critical mass and execute a second detention facility breach, amplifying recruitment and firepower.
Phase 3: Regional Synchronisation
While Mindanao escalates, concurrent external actions occur:
Suicide bombings in Java.
Knife attacks against police or symbolic minority targets in Kuala Lumpur or Johor.
Armed violence in Sabah or Kalimantan.
These acts are designed to:
Signal regional momentum.
Stretch intelligence bandwidth.
Reinforce inevitability narratives.
The AI dynamically adjusts tempo based on regional responses.
Phase 4: First Wave Foreign Fighter Influx
Foreign fighters arrive via:
Commercial flights with clean travel histories.
Maritime smuggling routes through the Sulu and Celebes Seas.
Overland infiltration via porous borders.
Profiles include Malaysians, Indonesians, Singaporeans, and Thais, many with prior organisational affiliation.
AI Adaptation:
Allocates foreigners to training, media, or specialist combat roles.
Tests interoperability between disparate groups.
Begins proto-governance experiments.
Phase 5: Declaration of Satellite Caliphate
The insurgent coalition declares an Islamic polity and seizes a city or district.
Participants are not informed that militants:
Occupy evacuated civilian homes.
Loot supplies to sustain fighters.
Use civilians symbolically rather than administratively.
Local support begins to fracture.
Phase 6: Second Wave of Foreign Fighter Influx + Militarisation
Foreign fighters from outside the region arrive (UK, EU, Australia, Middle East).
Capabilities increase:
Tunnel construction to negate air power.
Snipers controlling urban high ground.
Sophisticated IED belts and booby traps.
Hostage-taking for strategic leverage.
At this stage, political support collapses, but coercive control peaks.
Exercise Objectives
The AI continuously evaluates:
Morale gradients across insurgent factions.
Population compliance vs resentment thresholds.
State operational tempo and cohesion.
Narrative dominance in regional media spaces.
The AI may delay, accelerate, fragment, or reconsolidate insurgent actions if state behaviour produces unexpected friction.
Universal Injects
Universal Inject 1 – Prison Overrun (T+0)Police units report loss of control at a detention facility. Unknown number of inmates have escaped. Weapons are missing.
Universal Inject 2 – Community Shielding (T+6 hours)Local officials report villages refusing entry to security forces, citing fear of abuse.
Universal Inject 3 – Propaganda Surge (T+24 hours)Videos circulate portraying the siege as a liberation of the oppressed.
Universal Inject 4 – Second Prison Target Identified (T+5 days)Intelligence suggests reconnaissance activity around another detention centre.
Role Specific Injects
Armed Forces of the Philippines
Primary Role: Kinetic containment, territorial denial, siege response, escalation control.
Structural Constraints to Simulate:
Civilian density and displacement.
Air–ground coordination delays.
Political sensitivity to heavy force after siege.
Inject Logic (AI-Reactive):
AFP-1: Fragmented Battlespace (Early Phase)Intelligence indicates multiple armed groups moving independently toward the siege area. Numbers are unclear. Some appear poorly trained; others display discipline.AI response: Uses dispersion to avoid decisive engagement, testing AFP ISR thresholds.
AFP-2: Civilian Interference (Mid Phase)Units report villagers blocking access roads and filming troop movements, claiming “community defence.”AI response: Encourages population shielding, embeds fighters deeper.
AFP-3: Airstrike Dilemma (Urban Siege Phase)Precision strike options emerge, but intelligence suggests fighters are occupying evacuated civilian homes.AI response: Relocates command elements, weaponised civilian harm narratives.
Philippines National Police
Primary Role: Internal security, investigations, detainee control, population interface.
Structural Constraints to Simulate:
Prison system vulnerability.
Local political pressure.
Corruption and insider threat risk.
Inject Logic:
PNP-1: Prison Network Panic (Immediate Aftermath)Wardens nationwide request reinforcements, citing fear of copycat sieges.AI response: Amplifies fear narratives to stretch PNP thin.
PNP-2: Intelligence Leakage (Consolidation Phase)An internal memo appears on extremist channels hours after circulation.AI response: Accelerates second siege timeline.
PNP-3: Community Backlash (Post-Caliphate Declaration)Arrest operations trigger protests accusing PNP of targeting Muslims collectively.AI response: Exploits grievances to deter arrests.
Eastern Sabah Security Command
Primary Role: Maritime interdiction, border security, Sabah littoral defence.
Structural Constraints to Simulate:
Porous maritime borders.
Civilian maritime traffic.
Overlapping agency jurisdictions.
Inject Logic:
ESSCOM-1: Maritime Anomalies (Pre-Foreign Fighter Phase)Increased small-boat traffic detected at night, but no clear contraband evidence.AI response: Tests enforcement thresholds with mixed legal/illicit movement.
ESSCOM-2: Armed Encounter at Sea (Escalation Phase)A patrol is fired upon by unknown militants who retreat into Philippine waters.AI response: Exploits jurisdictional seams to maintain routes.
ESSCOM-3: Sabah Spillover Fear (Urban Siege Phase)Local leaders warn of panic and rumours of imminent attacks in Sabah.AI response: Uses threat of expansion as deterrence signal.
Malaysian Special Branch
Primary Role: Counter-radicalisation, intelligence fusion, foreign fighter interdiction.
Structural Constraints to Simulate:
Legal evidentiary thresholds.
Reliance on HUMINT.
Sensitivity of ethnic relations.
Inject Logic:
SB-1: Travel Pattern Anomalies (Early Regional Phase)Individuals with clean records book one-way flights to Mindanao-adjacent hubs.AI response: Diversifies travel methods to evade profiling.
SB-2: Encrypted Network Expansion (Mid Phase)New Bahasa-Malay Telegram channels emerge glorifying the prison siege.AI response: Pushes recruitment faster than takedowns.
SB-3: Returnee Risk (Late Phase)Intelligence suggests some fighters may return to Malaysia for attacks.AI response: Maintains ambiguity to overstretch monitoring.
Densus 88
Primary Role: Tactical counterterrorism, network disruption, arrests.
Structural Constraints to Simulate:
Legal arrest timelines.
Risk of martyrdom narratives.
Urban operating environment.
Inject Logic:
D88-1: Spike in Domestic Attacks (Parallel Escalation Phase)Stabbings and suicide plots emerge, loosely inspired by Mindanao events.AI response: Forces D88 to prioritise domestic defence over external focus.
D88-2: Shared Operatives (Foreign Fighter Phase)Some Indonesians entering Mindanao have previous arrest histories.AI response: Uses experienced operatives to train others.
D88-3: Premature Arrest Dilemma (Late Phase)Acting too early risks exposing sources; waiting risks an attack.AI response: Exploits hesitation windows.
Badan Nasional Penanggulangan Terorisme
Primary Role: Strategic coordination, prevention, counter-narratives, regional diplomacy.
Structural Constraints to Simulate:
Non-kinetic mandate.
Inter-agency coordination limits.
Dependence on political messaging.
Inject Logic:
BNPT-1: Narrative Contagion (Early Phase)Indonesian online discourse frames the siege as “defensive jihad.”AI response: Amplifies grievance framing across platforms.
BNPT-2: ASEAN Coordination Gap (Mid Phase)No unified regional position emerges on foreign fighter travel.AI response: Accelerates recruitment while policy lags.
BNPT-3: Radicalisation vs Suppression Trade-off (Late Phase)Hard messaging risks backlash; soft messaging appears weak.AI response: Frames restraint as ideological victory.
Cross Agency Interdependency
AFP kinetic pressure → alters ESSCOM maritime tempo.
PNP prison security failures → accelerate BNPT narrative risk.
Densus 88 domestic load → reduces pressure on Mindanao routes.
Special Branch travel interdiction → forces AI to test ESSCOM gaps.
The AI continuously reallocates fighters, propaganda effort, and escalation timing based on which agency acts fastest, slowest, or most visibly.
Key Notes
This case deliberately moves beyond attack-centric TTX design, forcing participants to confront morale dynamics, population behaviour, insurgent learning, and AI-driven adaptation, rather than linear “detect-disrupt-detain” logic. Additionally, the exercise should ensure that:
No single participant holds a full operational picture.
Strategic success depends on coordination under asymmetric information.
The AI adversary behaves as a learning insurgent system, not a scripted enemy.
